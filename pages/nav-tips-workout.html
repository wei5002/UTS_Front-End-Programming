Warming Up

Why do we need to warming up before exercise?

Warming up before exercise is a crucial step that many overlook, but it plays significant role in preparing the body for more intense physical activity. It gradually raises your heart rate, increases blood flow to the muscles, and enhances flexibility, which in turn can improve performance and injuries. By Warming up, you're allowing the muscles to loosen up and become more flexible, thus can reduce the risk of injuries such as muscle cramps, strains, or sprains. When you exercise without warming-up, your muscles are "cold" and stiff, making them more susceptible to injury.

Steps:
  • Light Jogging (3-5 minutes)
  • Arm Circles (3-5 minutes)
  • High Knees (2-3 minutes)

Arm Exercise

Strengthening your arms is not only for enhancing muscle definition but also for improving overall upper body strength. Arm exercises pla a vital role in building endurance and making daily activities like lifting, pushing, and pulling much easier. A regular arm workouts regimen not only builds muscle but also helps in boosting stamina and reducing the likelihood of injury by increasing the support for the upper body. Push-ups are one of the most effective and accessible exercises for developing arm strength, while also engaging other muscle groups such as the chest, shoulders, and core.

Steps:
  • Start in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower your body towards the ground while keeping your elbows close to your body.
  • Push through your palms to raise your body back to the starting position. (10-15 repetition)

Leg Exercise

Strong legs are fundamental component of overall physical fitness and endurance. Leg workouts can improve not only muscle strength but also balance, stability, and coordination. Developinh your leg muscles also enhances athletic performance, making activities such as running, jumping, or climbing more efficient. Squats are among the best exercises for targeting the lower body, as they work the major muscle groups, including quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Additionally, squats helps strengthen the core and improve posture, contributing to overall body functionality.

Steps:
  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with your toe spointing slightly outward.
  • Keep your arms at your sides or ectend them straight in front of you for balance.
  • Begin lowering your body by bending at the hips and knees as if you sitting back into a chair.
  • As you squat, make sure the weight is on you rheels and not your toes (10-15 repetition).

Back Exercise

A strong back is vital for maintaining good posture, balance, and overall strength. Including back exercises in your workout routine helps improve mobility and reduces the risk of injury, especially in the lower back. Strengthening back muscles can also alleviate back pain and imporve your posture, which is important for daily activities and sports performance. The bent-over row is an excellent exercise for targeting the upper back, shoulders, and arms, promoting a well-rounded upper body workout.

Steps:
  • Grab a weight and stand with feet hip-width apart, knees slightly bent.
  • Hinge at the hips, keeping your back straigth and let the weights hang down.
  • Pull the weight toward your ribs, keeping elbows close to your body.
  • Lower slowly back to the starting position, controlling the movement (8-12 repetition).

Chest Exercise

Building a strong chest not only enhances upper body strength but also improves your overall physical appearance and athletic performance. Chest exercises, such as the bench press, target the pectoral muscles, which play a crucial role in pushing movements. By strengthening these muscles, you improve your ability to perform tasks like pushing, lifting, or carrying heavy objects, while also boosting muscle mass and endurance in the upper body.

Steps:
  • Lie flat on a bench with your feet firmly on the ground. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Unrack the bar and slowly lower it towards you chest, keeping your elbows at 45 degrees angle.
  • Push the bar back up to the starting position by extending your arms fully without locking your elbows.
  • Continue the motion, focusing on control and maintaining proper form (8-12 repetition)

Abs Exercise

A strong core is essential for maintaining balance, stability, and overall strength. Abs exercises not only help sculpt your midsection but also support your posture and reduce the risk of injury during other workouts. One of the effective ways to strengthen tour core is through the plank exercise. The plank not only targets the abdominal muscles but also engages the shoulders, back, and glutes.

Steps:
  • Begin by getting into a push-up position with your forearms on the floor and elbows directly beneath your shoulders.
  • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, avoiding any sagging or arching in your back.
  • Tighten your abdominal muscles and hold this positioning, keeping your gaze downward.
  • Maintain the plank for 20-30 seconds, gradually increasing the time as you build strength. (3 sets of 20-30 seconds)

Cooling Down

Why do we need to cooling down after exercise?

Cooling down after exercise is just as important as warming up. It helps your body gradually return to its resting state by lowering your heart rate and blood pressure in a controlled manner. Cooling down also aids in preventing muscke stiffness and soreness by promoting blodd flow to remove waste products like lactic acid. Skipping this step could leave your muscles tense and fatigued, making recovery longer andincreasing the likelihood of post-exercise discomfort. Cooling down exercises are simple and can include stretches and light movements to help release muscle tension and improve flexibility.

Steps:
  • Walking (5-10 minutes)
  • Stretching (hold for 10-15 minutes)
  • Deep breathing (3-5 minutes)
